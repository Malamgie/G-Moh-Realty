5 Things Your Agent Won't Tell You About Buying Prime Land in Abuja

Posted by G Moh on September 25, 2025 | Land | 5 min read

I started G Moh Realty because I saw a massive trust gap. Many agents prioritize a quick commission over your long-term investment. My commitment is to honesty. Here are five crucial details about land purchases in Abuja you should demand your agent tells you.

1. The Real Cost of Title Perfection

The quoted price is rarely the final price. Many sellers or agents fail to factor in the true cost of perfecting the land title after purchase, which can run into millions. Always get a clear breakdown of the **Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)** process and costs before signing anything.

2. The "Fast Developing" Myth

Every land ad says 'fast developing.' The truth is often different. Check the actual infrastructure: is there power, water, or just a cleared road? A prime plot for us means existing or **fully funded infrastructure plans** you can verify.

3. Access Road Encroachment Risk

Many 'affordable' plots have access roads that are prone to future government reclamation or community disputes. We specialize in verifying the **original layout plans** with the relevant Abuja agencies to guarantee your access is secure and permanent.

4. The Hidden Tax Liabilities

Before closing, you must know if the land has accrued unpaid ground rents or property rates. If the seller hasn't paid, those liabilities become yours immediately. **We insist on verified tax clearance** as part of our transparent due diligence process.
